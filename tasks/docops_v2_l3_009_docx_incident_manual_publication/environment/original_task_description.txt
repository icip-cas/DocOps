Security, legal, and support teams often maintain incident response manuals as Word documents with draft headings, manual TOC placeholders, old section breaks, internal notes, privileged root-cause language, customer-facing tables, evidence appendices, and approval records all mixed together. The publication process is long and order-sensitive: internal material must be removed first, then public sections must be reorganized, TOC and page boundaries repaired, tables normalized, headers/footers updated, exceptions highlighted, and appendices split into a release-safe package.
